CARTOGRAPHIC MATERIALS


CLIENTELE:
Maps, atlases, digital geospatial data and cartographic reference books are acquired to serve the general cartographic requirements of the University community. The collection provides teaching and research materials for graduate and undergraduate students, faculty and staff, as well as the general public. Primary users are from the fields of architecture, urban planning, historic preservation, natural resources, history, and area studies.


EXISTING COLLECTION:
ECS Language W Atlases 3+; Maps 3+; Reference Sources 3+

CURRENT COLLECTION:
CCI Language W Atlases 3+; Maps 3+; Reference Sources 3+

SUBJECTS COVERED:
G(Geography), Z(Bibliography); some DA-DU, E, F(History), GA(Mathematical Geography – projections, etc.)


FORMATS:
Because of the varied informational needs that are to be met by the map collection, a wide variety of materials are acquired, including topographic and thematic sheet maps, digital geospatial data in standard GIS, raster or vector formats, city plans, base and outline maps, aerial photographs of local and international urban areas, navigation charts, regional, national and thematic atlases, bibliographies, directories, technical manuals, dictionaries, biographical works, gazetteers, globes, relief maps. Early works are acquired only in facsimile.


CHRONOLOGY:
Although emphasis is on contemporary publications, material portraying all periods is collected.

GOVERNMENT DOCUMENTS:
Depository maps from the Army Map Service (retrospective international topographic coverage) , the U.S. Geological Survey (retrospective and current national coverage), NOAA and NIMA (current nautical charts covering national and international waters) comprise roughly 50% of the collection. Depository digital geospatial data on CD and DVD from the Bureau of the Census, the USGS and other producing agencies are collected. Three graphics and GIS workstations are equipped to access and manipulate online geospatial data. Much of the international material described above is government document material.


EXCLUSIONS:
Digital satellite data (collected at SPIF, Space Sciences Building); historic and current NYS aerial photography coverages (paper - IRIS, Rice Hall; DOQs – NYS and other G.I.S. Clearinghouses); rare and manuscript materials (Rare & Manuscript Collections, Kroch Library); soil surveys (Mann Library), Icelandic maps (Icelandic Collection, Kroch Library), linguistic atlases (Olin stacks); Web accessible public domain New York State geospatial data and metadata (CUGIR, Mann Library); digital data with unreasonable restrictions, such as those which restrict use to only a part or parts of the Cornell Community, are not acquired.
