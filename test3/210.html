Online databases

“I love that I have easy online access to databases through the Library’s Web site – especially PubMed and Web of Science, which I use for my research on behavioral variations in crickets. I can search for journal articles wherever I am, from home or on campus.

Librarian office hours

“If you have an in-depth, detailed question, you can go to one of the offices and have one-on-one time with a librarian – and if you need specific resources, they’ll help you directly without you having to try to figure it out on your own. It really saves time. Librarians have a lot of knowledge about the databases and resources we have, and they know what will be best to help.”

PDF scanner in Mann

“In the rare event that the Library doesn’t have the online edition of a journal I need, I try to find the hard copy of the article and then use the scanner in Mann to create a PDF. It’s easy to use and I don’t have to print it off, so I save money and time.

Current periodicals

“With Web-based search engines, there’s a tendency to focus on one small area of scholarship in your research. But if you come to the current periodicals section, you have an array of materials you might not otherwise look at and is easily accessible. It could also introduce you to journals you might not otherwise see, so then you can investigate further.”

Bollywood collection

"The dynamic new face of South Asian studies! It started out as serious, hard-core piles of manuscripts, but now the world knows about India and Bollywood is carrying it. It's a part of comparative literature these days; it’s rare to read a scholarly paper on India that doesn’t make reference to film. It’s colorful, it’s singing and dancing, it’s the New India – and it's at the Library."

Renovated learning spaces in Mann

“We have a classroom for workshops – specifically, to teach workshops and class sections on everything you need to know to do research fast and effectively. In the new Bissett Collaborative Center on the second floor of Mann, if people have group projects, they can pull together anything they need on the fly: chairs, tables, computer equipment, mobile technology, plasma screens.

Passkey

"When I'm off-campus and trying to get into JSTOR or Proquest or any of the licensed databases, all I have to do is click the Passkey icon on my browser. I'm automatically prompted for my Cornell credentials, which lets me into the database in one quick step. It saves a lot of time and effort!"

Rare Books and Manuscript Collection

"The Library system is a really great resource for physical texts, in addition to the online collections. In one class, I had to go to RMC in Kroch and look through a facscimile of the Book of Kells. It's amazing visually, and to have the copy physically in front of you instead of just on a computer was such a connection.
