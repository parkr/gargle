Italian History

Clientele: The primary clientele for this collection is the faculty and students (graduate and undergraduate) in the History Department. Other interested parties are those working on Italian and Renaissance Literature.

Existing Collection: ECS 4; LANGUAGE W
The Italian History collection is strong, containing works on all periods and including the published papers and memoirs of prominent individuals. The major national and regional journals are represented.

Current Collecting: CCI 4; LANGUAGE W
All periods of Italian History are covered from 1200 to the present. Periods before 1200 are collected by the Medieval Bibliographer and the selector for Classics and Ancient History. Particular emphasis is placed on the Renaissance, the Risorgimento of the Nineteenth Century, and the Fascist period of the Twentieth Century. The major national and regional journals are included.

Subjects Covered: LC classes DG 401-583, DG 670-684.72, DG 791-800, DG 803-817.3, DG 861-869, DG 987-999, DF 701-951

Geographical Information: There are no geographical limitations on imprints, however, most items are published in Italy, Western Europe, and the United States.

Exclusions: 1. Juvenile literature
2. Genealogy
3. Manuscripts and archives
4. Rare books
5. Newspapers
6. Works dealing with the period before 1200
7. Textbooks

Notes: The history of modern Greece is purchased on this fund.
