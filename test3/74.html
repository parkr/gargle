General History

Clientele: The primary clientele for this collection is the faculty and students (graduate and undergraduate) in the History Department. Other disciplines for which this collection may have relevance include Peace Studies, Sociology, and Literature.

Existing Collection: ECS 3-; LANGUAGE W

This collection is strong on World Wars I and II with published official records and many memoirs of important participants. Another strength is modern Western Europe; there are a number of major journals and multi-volume sets in this area.

Current Collecting: CCI 3; LANGUAGE W

Collecting in this area focuses on the history of Western Europe from 1485 to the present. The period prior to 1485 is covered by the Medieval Bibliographer and the Ancient History and Classical History selector. Works having to do with more than one Western European country are included. Major journals and multi-volume sets are collected; large microform sets have not been purchases recently due to budget constraints. Items dealing with Eastern Europe are selected by the Slavic Bibliographer. Histories of the Jewish Holocaust of World War II are selected by the Jewish Studies Bibliographer.

Subjects Covered: LC classes D 204-475, D 501-68, D731-838, D839-850, and D 901-1075

Geographical Information: There is no limit on the geographical location of imprints. However, most items are published in the United States and Western Europe.

Exclusions:

1. Juvenile literature
2. Genealogy
3. Manuscripts and archives
4. Rare Books
5. Newspapers
6. Items dealing with the period before 1485
7. Eastern Europe
8. Developing countries
9. Jewish Holocaust
10. Textbooks

Notes: For post-World War II Western Europe coordination with the Western European Social Science Bibliographer is required.
